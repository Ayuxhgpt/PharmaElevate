What is Pharmacy?

Pharmacy is the branch of health sciences that deals with the discovery, production, safe use, and regulation of medicines. It combines aspects of chemistry, biology, and medicine. Pharmacists ensure that patients receive the correct medicines in the right dosage, educate about drug use, and safeguard public health.

×

Why Pharmacy Matters

Pharmacy is more than dispensing drugs — it ensures safe and effective use of medications, manages drug interactions, prevents misuse, and supports doctors in treatment plans. Pharmacists play a vital role in public health campaigns, chronic disease management, and guiding patients towards better outcomes.
